# Customer Discovery Questions

Use this guide for your 20-30 validation calls. Your goal: understand if this is a real problem worth solving.

## Before the Call

**Research:**
- Check their website/product
- Note their target market (countries, use case)
- Estimate their scale (traffic, users)

**Book 20-30 min max**

## Opening (2 min)

```
"Thanks for taking the time! I'm building a low-cost, low-bandwidth Video KYC solution and want to make sure I'm solving a real problem.

I have 8 questions - should take about 20 minutes. Sound good?"
```

## Section 1: Current Situation (5 min)

### Q1: KYC Setup
"Walk me through your current KYC process. What provider do you use?"

**Listen for:**
- Provider name (Jumio, Onfido, Sumsub, etc.)
- In-house vs third-party
- Pain points they mention

---

### Q2: Volume & Cost
"How many verifications do you do per month? What does it cost per verification?"

**Listen for:**
- Monthly volume (helps with pricing)
- Cost per verification ($2-5 is typical)
- Whether cost is a major concern

---

### Q3: Drop-off Rate
"What percentage of users who start KYC actually complete it?"

**Listen for:**
- Specific number (30-60% is common)
- Whether they track this metric
- Whether they care about it

---

### Q4: Geographic Breakdown
"Where are most of your users located? Any specific countries with high failure rates?"

**Listen for:**
- Emerging markets (Africa, LATAM, SE Asia)
- Network quality issues
- Language barriers

---

## Section 2: Pain Points (8 min)

### Q5: Biggest Frustration
"What's the #1 thing that frustrates you most about your current KYC setup?"

**Listen for:**
- Cost
- Drop-off rates
- User experience
- Compliance issues
- Technical limitations

---

### Q6: Drop-off Reasons
"When users fail or abandon KYC, what are the main reasons?"

**Listen for:**
- "Poor internet connection" ← YOUR KEY INSIGHT
- "Process too complicated"
- "Document issues"
- "Don't trust us"

---

### Q7: What Would Make It Better
"If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about KYC, what would it be?"

**Listen for:**
- Specific feature requests
- Willingness to switch providers
- Budget sensitivity

---

## Section 3: Solution Validation (5 min)

### Q8: Pitch Your Solution
"I'm working on a Video KYC that:
- Works on 2G networks (extreme low-bandwidth mode)
- Costs $0.99-$1.49 (vs $3-5 from big providers)
- Supports 15 languages
- Same enterprise security/compliance

Would this solve a problem for you?"

**Listen for:**
- Genuine excitement vs polite interest
- Specific concerns or objections
- Questions about pricing/features

---

### Q9: Willingness to Pay
"If this reduced your drop-off rate by 50%, what would you be willing to pay per verification?"

**Listen for:**
- Price anchoring
- Whether cost is the main driver
- Willingness to switch

---

### Q10: Beta Interest
"I'm looking for 3-5 companies to beta test this. I'd give you 1,000 free verifications + customize anything you need. In exchange, I'd love your feedback and a testimonial if it works well.

Would you be interested?"

**Listen for:**
- Immediate "yes" (HOT LEAD)
- "Maybe" or conditions (WARM LEAD)
- "No" or deflection (NOT A FIT)

---

## Closing (2 min)

### If Interested:
```
"Awesome! Let me send you a quick Loom demo + beta agreement. 

One last question: Who else should I talk to about this? Anyone in your network dealing with similar issues?"
```

### If Not Interested:
```
"No worries at all - appreciate your time!

Before we go: is there anyone you know who might be a better fit for this? Especially folks with:
- Global user base
- High KYC drop-off rates
- Cost sensitivity"
```

---

## After the Call

**Immediately:**
1. Send thank-you email (within 5 min)
2. Update customer tracker with notes
3. Categorize: HOT / WARM / COLD / NOT A FIT

**Hot Lead:**
- Expressed clear interest in beta
- Has the problem you're solving
- Gave specific pricing feedback

**Warm Lead:**
- Interested but needs more convincing
- Has the problem but not urgent
- Wants to "circle back"

**Follow-up within 24 hours:**
- Hot: Send demo + beta terms
- Warm: Send case study or more info
- Cold: Add to nurture list (monthly check-ins)

---

## Red Flags (Not a Good Fit)

❌ "We're happy with our current provider" + no pain points
❌ "Cost isn't really an issue for us"
❌ Low volume (<100 verifications/month)
❌ No decision-making authority
❌ "Send me more info" (deflection)

---

## Green Flags (Great Fit)

✅ Mentions network/bandwidth issues unprompted
✅ Talks about cost multiple times
✅ Asks specific technical questions
✅ Wants to introduce you to their team
✅ Asks "When can we start?"

---

## Goal Metrics

After 20-30 calls, you should have:
- **5-10 hot leads** (want to beta test)
- **10-15 warm leads** (interested, need nurturing)
- **Clear pattern** in pain points (validates your solution)
- **Pricing validation** (they'd pay $0.99-$1.49)
- **3-5 beta customers** committed

## Time Investment

- **Per call:** 30-40 min (20 min call + 10 min prep/notes)
- **Daily goal:** 2-3 calls
- **Weekly goal:** 10-15 calls
- **Total:** 20-30 calls over 2-3 weeks

**This is the most important work you'll do. Don't skip this.**
